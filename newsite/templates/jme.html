J. Morris “Morrie” Evans (1921-2017) was a member of Haverford College’s class of 1943. He donated the entirety of the collection to Haverford College Special Collections. The collection is comprised primarily of letters but also includes diaries and financial documents. Evans last donated a batch of documents which was approved in June 2019. Some of these items are available digitally. This addition includes letters received and sent by Evans himself, shipping and financial documents, and letters from the nineteenth century. The shipping documents illuminate some of the Cope Evans business patterns.

In his free time, Evans compiled documents concerning the Cope Evans and kept track of the documents he created including family photographs and letters. He compiled and examined more than 2,600 documents (letters, records and diaries).

Evans was a conscientious objector during World War II. He worked on farms as alternative service. After the war, he began work as an accountant at the Philadelphia Quartz company which later became the PQ corporation. J. Morris Evans was subsequently Vice-President and and Treasurer of the Corporation.
